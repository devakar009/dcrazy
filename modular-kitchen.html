Modular Kitchen

Modular Kitchen is a modern kitchen layout that contains modules of cabinet units that include
wall unit, floor unit, tall storage units, wooden cabinets, countertop, chimney hob,
built-in-oven etc.
There are different types of modular kitchens:

Straight Kitchen

Best suited for small kitchens with an open plan, the straight kitchen is a linear layout
that does not employ the work triangle. It optimizes efficient use of your space with a
compact and unobstructed workflow design. In this arrangement, regularly used appliances and
provisions can be stored on the countertop and the cabinets above and below can be used to
stow away lesser used products.

U-Shaped Kitchen

As the name suggests, the layout has a counter running on three sides of the kitchen, leaving
one open. This type of kitchen works well for spaces of all sizes. The floor plan has two large
counters and a small one, allowing for more than two people to move around without obstructing the
workflow.

L-Shaped Kitchen

The L-shaped kitchen is ideal for small and open kitchen layouts. It can be adapted into any type
of house; the two arms can be elongated or shortened, keeping in mind the structure of the kitchen
and availability of space. You can also convert the extra space into a dining nook or install a
kitchen island to store additional cooking equipment.

Parallel Kitchen

The parallel kitchen is possibly the most efficient. With two working areas facing each other,
workspaces can be split into ‘wet’ and ‘dry’ areas, making it convenient for more than one person
to move around. Also, the cabinets run both on the top and bottom on either side, making way for plenty
of storage space.
